Key General Knowledge Areas

General Knowledge is a crucial component of most government examinations. It covers a vast array of subjects designed to test a candidate's awareness of the world around them. Mastering GK requires consistent reading and staying updated with current events.

  • Indian History: Ancient, Medieval, and Modern Indian History, including key events, movements, and personalities.
  • Indian Geography: Physical features, climate, rivers, agriculture, industries, and population.
  • Indian Polity & Constitution: Structure and functioning of the Indian Constitution, Preamble, Fundamental Rights, Duties, Directive Principles, Parliament, Judiciary, etc.
  • Indian Economy: Basic concepts, economic planning, sectors of the economy, inflation, banking, and international organizations.
  • General Science: Basic concepts of Physics, Chemistry, and Biology, often focusing on everyday applications.
  • Current Affairs: National and International events, awards, sports, important dates, government schemes, etc.
  • Art & Culture: Indian classical dances, music, festivals, architecture, and famous personalities.
  • Computer Knowledge: Basic computer concepts, hardware, software, internet, and common applications.

Regularly practicing quizzes and reading newspapers are highly recommended for improving your GK score.
